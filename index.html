Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Your helmet should be replaced if it shows any signs of wear or damage, or if it has been involved in an accident. Additionally, it's recommended to replace helmets every 5 years as the materials degrade over time.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

If your bike has been in storage for a while, it's a good idea to check the tires, brakes, and chain before riding. Make sure everything is in working order and properly lubricated.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, always follow local traffic laws, use hand signals when turning, and be mindful of pedestrians. Wearing a helmet is also highly recommended for safety.
